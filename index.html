Dear Suchir,

I've been thinking about what to say since the moment I knew you were leaving. And somehow everything I rehearsed felt too polished, too neat — so I'm just going to say it the way I actually mean it.

You got into Atlassian. Atlassian. I hope you know what that means — not just the name of the company, but what it says about you. You worked for this quietly, consistently, without making a big thing of it. That's very you. And that's one of the thousand things I love about you.

I am so proud of you it's almost annoying. Like, genuinely, unreasonably proud. The kind where I want to tell strangers about it.

Three years, Suchir. That's not a small thing. That's figuring each other out through exams and bad days and the in-between moments that don't make great stories but make up most of a life. I'd choose all of it again without blinking.

The distance is going to be annoying, I'm not going to pretend otherwise. I'll probably reach for my phone at weird hours. I'll probably miss your stupid smile at the most random moments — when something funny happens, when I'm eating something good, when I'm just tired and want to sit next to someone who gets it.

But here's what I keep coming back to: this is supposed to be hard. It means something that it's hard. And two months is two months. Not forever.

When it gets overwhelming over there — and it might, because new cities, new people, new pressure are a lot all at once — I just want you to remember that you're not starting from zero. You have already proven, to me and to yourself, that you're capable of more than you think. Every single time you've doubted yourself, you've come out the other side fine. More than fine.

You're doing better than you think. I need you to know that.

Your smile, by the way, is still my favourite thing. I just wanted to say that somewhere you could read it.

Go be great. Go learn things and meet people and eat weird food and stay up too late and come back full of stories. I want to hear all of it.

But also — drink water. Sleep. Call me.

— your Golu
